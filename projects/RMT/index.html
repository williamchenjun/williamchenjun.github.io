Random Matrix Theory and the Riemann Zeta Function

PDF


Description

Academic paper dealing with one of the most reknown concepts in Mathematics. The Riemann zeta function is widely used in all scientific fields, however, one baffling problem is yet to be solved. In 1859, Bernhard Riemann would propose a hypothesis that would remain unsolved and frustrate mathematicians. The importance of the Riemann hypothesis is given by its connection to the nature of prime numbers and their distribution.
This paper is an exposition on the recent research made by Keating, Jon P., and Nina C. Snaith, on the connection between the distribution of the spacing between consecutive (ordered) eigenvalues of random matrices and the distribution of the spacing between consecutive zeros of the Riemann zeta function.
